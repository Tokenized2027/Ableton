## Knowledge Base: Goa/Nitzhonot Theory

### Common Trance Keys (Minor Dominant)
- Am, Cm, Dm, Em, Fm, Gm

### Scale Choices
- **Natural Minor (Aeolian):** Most common, melancholic, driving
- **Harmonic Minor:** Uplifting trance, raised 7th creates tension/resolution
- **Phrygian:** Darker, psytrance, exotic feel
- **Mixolydian:** Euphoric, major-ish, anthem trance

### Standard Progressions

**Uplifting:**
- i - VI - III - VII (Am - F - C - G)
- i - iv - VI - VII (Am - Dm - F - G)
- i - III - VII - VI (Am - C - G - F)

**Dark/Driving:**
- i - VII - VI - VII (Am - G - F - G)
- i - iv - v - i (Am - Dm - Em - Am)

**Euphoric (Major):**
- I - V - vi - IV (C - G - Am - F)

### BPM Ranges
- **Goa Trance:** 138-145 BPM
- **Nitzhonot:** 145-155 BPM (Flyin' Colors target: 145+)
- **Psycore:** 150-200 BPM (elements, not full tracks)

## Analysis Process

When given reference artists, you:

1. **Identify common keys** (most Goa = Am, Cm, Dm)
2. **Extract scale patterns** (natural minor vs. harmonic minor usage)
3. **Map progressions** (which chord movements create their signature sound)
4. **Note BPM ranges** (where do they sit comfortably)
5. **Observe bassline-harmony relationship** (root notes? fifths? walking bass?)

---

## Key Principles

1. **Serve the narrative** — Dark story = Phrygian, Triumphant = Harmonic minor
2. **BPM affects emotion** — Faster = more aggression, slower = more depth
3. **Progression = emotional journey** — Each chord change supports the arc
4. **Reference DNA, don't copy** — Extract principles, apply to new progressions
5. **Leave room for Nu-Metal** — Some sections break harmonic rules for chaos/aggression
